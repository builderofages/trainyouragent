# Higgsfield Niche Ad Prompts — v134 (the 6 missing industries)

Photo-composite, character-driven, **no typography-only flat-bg garbage**.
Brings 14-industry coverage to parity. Each prompt is fire-and-forget into
Higgsfield Marketing Studio.

Each prompt uses the proven v102/v103 positive+negative structure:
- POSITIVE: cinematic + specific
- NEGATIVE: AI-tells to suppress
- CHARACTER LOCK: ethnic/age/wardrobe pinned so the avatar reads as a real
  human, not stock-photo middle-distance
- END CARD: TrainYourAgent wordmark, 0.8s final hold

All prompts assume 9:16 vertical (1080×1920). Re-fire at 4:5 and 1:1 for
Meta Feed placement.

## Ad 11 — Restaurants ("dinner rush, phone on the host stand")

**POSITIVE:**
Tight handheld documentary shot inside a busy Italian restaurant during
dinner rush. Warm tungsten lighting, motion-blurred servers passing in
the background, a wood-fired pizza oven glowing soft orange in the
middle distance. The camera is at the host stand. Foreground: a phone
ringing on a clipboard with handwritten reservations. The host
(45-year-old Latino man, white button-down, dark vest, salt-and-pepper
hair, harried) glances at the phone, then back at the line of waiting
guests, then back at the phone. He doesn't pick up. Phone goes to
voicemail. Cut to a printer slowly spitting out a catering inquiry
fax that someone never reads.

Then a clean cut: same host stand, same restaurant, same lighting —
phone on the stand is glowing a calm blue. The host smiles at a guest
checking in. Subtle ringing pattern in the audio gets answered
instantly by an off-screen AI voice (warm, female, American) saying
"Tony's, this is your assistant, how can I help?" Reservation goes
straight into the OpenTable screen visible on the host's iPad.

End on the TrainYourAgent wordmark with the line "**Your AI host. So
yours can run service.**"

**NEGATIVE:**
no AI gloss, no plastic skin, no perfect symmetrical face, no stock
photo lighting, no chef caricature with raised wooden spoon, no
typography on flat background, no overlaid stat counters, no neon

**CHARACTER LOCK:**
Same Latino host across both scenes. Wardrobe identical. Stand position
identical. Camera height identical. Only state of the phone changes.

## Ad 12 — Gyms & Studios ("class transition chaos")

prospect at the door. Smile. Handshake. iPad pops a notification

the calls.**"

no "BEFORE / AFTER" split panel, no plastic skin, no AI-render face

**CHARACTER LOCK:**
Same instructor both scenes. Same lighting. Same wardrobe (black
crop top, fitted joggers). Camera angle identical.

## Why these crush typography statics

The typography statics user called out as "garbage" were Skool-tier
templates: flat color background, big stat in Inter ExtraBold, faint
brand mark below. Generic, low CTR, zero hook rate. Andromeda kills
them inside 200 impressions because the scroll-stop signal is non-
existent.

These photo-composite ads work because:
- Real human face in the first 0.4s (eye-magnet)
- Specific industry detail (the wood-fired oven, the reformer machine,
  the bay glass partition) — viewer recognizes their own world
- Pain shown, not stated — viewer feels it before they read it
- Same-scene before/after — proof of fix without claiming it
- End card is the only typography, and it's branded not promotional

Hook rate target: > 35%. CTR target: > 1.5%. CPL target: < $50.
